🧠 Most business problems aren't problems at all.

They're symptoms of system dysfunction.

A manufacturing CEO cut quality inspection staff to save costs. Within six months, complaints doubled, returns spiked, and they lost their largest account.

The "cost saving" created a cascade that cost 10x the saved amount.

This isn't about poor execution. It's about poor systems thinking.

In this guide, I break down:
→ The 5 core principles of systems thinking
→ How to map your business system (with examples)
→ Finding leverage points where small changes create big impact
→ Common archetypes that trap organizations
→ Mental shifts from linear to systems thinking

Leaders who understand this distinction don't just solve problems—they prevent them from emerging.
